Scale of the Stakes

The U.S. commercial aviation industry represents $1.45 trillion in economic output and supports 10 million jobs. Yet Boeing alone reported a loss of $11.8 billion in 2024 due to operational failures and quality issues.

And in July 2024, Boeing pleaded guilty to federal criminal fraud charges for deliberately deceiving the FAA during the certification of the 737 Max, agreeing to pay up to $487 million in fines.

These are not isolated events. They are symptoms of a system that has not kept pace with the complexity of modern aircraft manufacturing.

How Cognitive QA Works

The inspector sees it.
In the moment it matters.

Boeing / Assembly Line

Cognitive QA puts a HoloLens on the engineer and overlays a live digital twin of the aircraft blueprint onto the physical plane in front of them. The AI scans in real time and flags deviations, damage, and defects spatially anchored to the exact part of the aircraft where the issue exists.

The inspector does not have to guess or cross-reference a paper checklist. They see it.

Defects caught before a panel is sealed cost a fraction of what they cost to fix after the fact, and a fraction of a fraction of what they cost if they reach a passenger. Cognitive QA catches those defects in the moment the work is happening, not after a sign-off, not after an audit, not after an incident.

MRO Companies / Maintenance

For MRO companies, the value is speed, consistency, and certainty.

Every inspector, regardless of experience level, performs at the level of your most senior one.

The HoloLens overlay eliminates the gap between a 30-year veteran and a technician on their second year. The AI carries the institutional knowledge. The human executes with full confidence.

The Unbroken Chain

Cognitive QA does not just close one of those gaps. It creates a single, unbroken, tamper-proof chain of verified truth that every party in that chain can rely on.

Boeing's verification travels to the FAA. The FAA's record travels to the airline. The airline's history travels to MRO. For the first time, quality does not have to be rediscovered at every step.

It travels with the aircraft.
