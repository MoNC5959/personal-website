The Problem We Are Solving

Aviation does not have a safety
ambition problem. It has a process problem.

Boeing, MRO companies, airlines, and regulators all want the same outcome. An aircraft that is safe to fly. But each of them has to verify that independently, from scratch, because no shared, trustworthy record of quality has ever existed.

THE STRUCTURAL FAILURE
Four handoffs. Four gaps. Four opportunities to miss something.
CRITICAL

Alaska Airlines 1282

4 BOLTS

Left the factory missing four critical bolts. A door plug failed at altitude over Oregon.

WARNING

LATAM Dreamliner

50 INJURED

Dropped without warning mid-flight. No alert. No time to react.

CRITICAL

Jeju Air 2216

179 LIVES

Crashed on December 29th, 2024. 179 of 181 people on board did not survive.

These incidents did not happen because people stopped caring. They happened because the process was not built to catch what human eyes can miss. The current approach to commercial aircraft inspection is built on manual processes, paper checklists, and individual sign-offs. It has no mechanism for creating a continuous, verifiable record of quality across the full lifecycle of an aircraft.

That is the gap Cognitive QA was built to close.

What Cognitive QA Replaces

Not an upgrade to the old process.
A replacement of it.

Today, inspectors work from printed or digital checklists, verify components by eye, and sign off based on training and experience. When the aircraft moves from one party to the next, that record does not move with it. Cognitive QA replaces that process entirely.

Proven Where It Matters Most

Defense grade.
Commercial scale.

The Microsoft HoloLens 2 is not untested in aviation. Boeing has already deployed it in partnership with the government to improve defense aircraft manufacturing. Airbus is moving toward AR-assisted inspection on the commercial side. The technology has proven itself in the most demanding, high-stakes environments the industry has to offer.

But there is a gap. That same proven technology has never been applied to commercial aircraft manufacturing. The aircraft that carry millions of passengers every single day are still being inspected with manual processes that defense aviation left behind years ago.

Defense aircraft operate under some of the strictest quality standards in the world. Every component verified. Every system tested. Every deviation documented. Commercial aviation deserves that same standard. Not because regulators require it. Because the public depends on it.

Cognitive QA brings the rigor of defense-grade quality control to the commercial manufacturing floor for the first time.
